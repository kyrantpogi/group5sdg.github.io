Guernica

Author: Pablo Picasso

The physical characteristic of Picasso’s “Guernica” created in 1937 includes a pyramidal composition featuring muted colors and intense motifs, symbolizing the barbarity and terror of war through its depiction of animals, including a bull and a wounded horse, alongside human figures, such as a grieving mother and a dead soldier, alluding to the devastation of modern society and advocating for peace, justice, and strong institutions in the face of conflict and tragedy
